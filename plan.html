Courier-Based Repair Service
Business Plan & Startup Guide

Executive Summary

This business plan outlines the launch of a courier-based PC and laptop repair service in Limerick, Ireland. The business leverages low overhead costs, technical expertise, and a differentiated service model to serve residential and small business customers across Limerick city and county. With minimal startup capital (under €1,000) and a flexible home-based operation, this venture can begin as a part-time operation and scale based on demand.

Market Analysis

Market Trends & Opportunity

The global electronic equipment repair service market is experiencing robust growth, valued at approximately USD 142.6 billion in 2024 and projected to reach USD 294.8 billion by 2035, representing a compound annual growth rate of 6.8%. The computer hardware repair segment specifically was valued at USD 6.4 billion in 2024 and is expected to reach USD 10.6 billion by 2033 (6.5% CAGR).

Several key trends support this growth in 2025-2026:

  • Rising component costs (DDR, NAND) making repairs more economical than replacement
  • Growing environmental consciousness driving repair-over-replace decisions
  • Extended device lifecycles as consumers keep computers longer
  • Remote work increasing reliance on functional home computing equipment
  • E-waste regulations encouraging repair and refurbishment

Business Model & Operations

Value Proposition

  • Convenience: Courier pickup and delivery eliminates customer travel and scheduling hassles
  • Expertise: 10+ years IT experience, L3 technical support background, Windows/Linux proficiency
  • Transparency: Clear pricing, honest assessments, free diagnostics option
  • Flexibility: Service area covers entire Limerick region, not limited to city center

Operations Structure

The business operates from a home-based workshop, eliminating retail lease costs. Standard workflow proceeds as follows:

Step Action Timeline
1. Booking Customer books via website/phone Immediate
2. Pickup Courier collects device from customer Within 24 hours
3. Diagnosis Technical assessment and quote 24-48 hours
4. Approval Customer approves work and cost Customer dependent
5. Repair Complete repair and testing 1-3 days (issue dependent)
6. Return Courier delivers to customer Same day after completion

Launch Action Plan

Phase 1: Legal & Administrative Setup (Week 1-2)

  • Register as a sole trader with Revenue Commissioners
  • Obtain tax registration number (self-employment registration)
  • Purchase public liability insurance (minimum €1 million coverage)
  • Open dedicated business bank account
  • Consult with accountant on tax obligations and record-keeping
  • Register business name if using a trading name

Phase 2: Infrastructure & Tools (Week 2-3)

  • Audit existing tools and identify any gaps
  • Purchase essential additional tools if needed
  • Build small initial parts inventory (cables, thermal paste, common components)
  • Set up dedicated workspace in home
  • Create work order and customer agreement templates
  • Establish relationships with parts suppliers (local and online)

Phase 3: Courier & Logistics (Week 3-4)

  • Research courier options: DPD, Fastway, An Post, local couriers
  • Obtain quotes for pickup/delivery within Limerick area
  • Set up courier accounts (may start pay-as-you-go)
  • Determine courier cost pass-through or inclusion in pricing
  • Establish packaging standards for safe device transport
  • Create customer instructions for preparing devices for courier pickup

Phase 4: Online Presence (Week 3-5)

  • Design and build professional website with clear service descriptions
  • Implement online booking system or contact form
  • Create pricing calculator or transparent pricing table
  • Set up Google My Business listing with accurate service area
  • Optimize website for local SEO (Limerick-focused keywords)
  • Create business profiles on relevant directories
  • Design simple logo and basic branding materials
  • Set up business email address and phone number

Phase 5: Marketing Launch (Week 5-6)

  • Develop initial marketing strategy and budget
  • Create Google Ads campaign targeting local searches
  • Post in local Facebook groups and community pages
  • Consider targeted ads in local newspapers or community newsletters
  • Offer launch promotion (e.g., free diagnostic with first repair)
  • Network with local businesses that might need IT support
  • Ask friends/family to spread word and leave initial reviews
  • Create simple flyers for community boards

Phase 6: Soft Launch (Week 6-8)

  • Begin accepting first customers (start with 2-3 jobs to test workflow)
  • Refine processes based on initial feedback
  • Document time spent on each job type for future pricing adjustments
  • Collect customer feedback and testimonials
  • Test courier logistics and timing
  • Identify any process bottlenecks or inefficiencies
  • Adjust pricing if necessary based on actual costs

Phase 7: Scale & Optimize (Month 3+)

  • Gradually increase marketing budget based on ROI
  • Build customer database and implement follow-up system
  • Consider offering referral incentives
  • Evaluate hiring friend for courier runs if volume justifies
  • Track key metrics: customer acquisition cost, average job value, repeat rate
  • Expand service offerings based on customer demand
  • Consider business insurance upgrades if needed
  • Plan for tax obligations (quarterly if necessary)

Key Success Factors

Critical Principles for Long-Term Success

Be Brutally Honest

If a device isn't worth repairing, tell the customer. If you can't fix something, admit it. Your reputation for honesty will generate more business than any upsell ever could.

Deliver on Promises

When you quote a turnaround time, meet it. When you quote a price, stick to it unless you discover something new (and communicate that immediately).

Communicate Proactively

Keep customers updated at every stage. Text or email when device arrives, when diagnosis is complete, when repair is done, when courier is dispatched.

Invest in Quality

Use quality replacement parts. A cheap screen that fails in 6 months destroys your reputation. Warranty your work properly.

Never Stop Learning

Technology evolves constantly. Stay current with common issues, new hardware, software changes. Join technician forums and communities.

Build a Safety Net

Keep 3-6 months of expenses saved. Computer repair can be seasonal and unpredictable. Don't quit your day job until you're confident in consistent demand.

Document Everything

Detailed work orders, before/after photos, customer agreements. Protects you legally and helps track what works.

Price for Sustainability

Don't compete on price alone. You're offering convenience and expertise. Price accordingly and don't race to the bottom.

Know When to Say No

Don't take jobs beyond your expertise. Don't work with unreasonable customers. Your time and mental health matter.

Think Long-Term

Every customer interaction is building (or destroying) your reputation. Play the long game.
